Scientific Activities

I am particularly interested in developping new mathematical tools to better understand and/or simulate physical systems.
My PhD works are at the crossroads of Homogenization Theory and Inverse Problem. More precisely, C. Le Bris, F. Legoll and myselft are developping strategies based on Homogenization Theory to reconstruct the effective properties associated to a multiscale PDE based on measurement of their solutions. It finds applications e.g. in Mechanics (see composite materials).
During my internship at EDF Lab Chatou, M. Ferrand, J.-M. Hérard, T. Norddine and myself developped new numerical schemes to simulate turbulent incompressible flows.
During my internship at CEA Grenoble, I. Duchemin, L. Genovese, E. Letournel, A. Levitt, and myself developped a new method to compute the eigenvalues of a Schrödinger operator.
