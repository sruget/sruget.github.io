Research Activities

Activités de recherche

I am particularly interested in developping new mathematical tools to better understand and/or simulate physical systems.
My PhD works are at the crossroads of Homogenization Theory and Inverse Problem. More precisely, Claude Le Bris, Frédéric Legoll and I are developping strategies based on Homogenization Theory to reconstruct the effective properties associated to a multiscale PDE based on measurement of their solutions. It finds applications e.g. in Mechanics (see composite materials).
During my internship at EDF Lab Chatou, Martin Ferrand, Jean-Marc Hérard, Thomas Norddine and I developped new numerical schemes to simulate turbulent incompressible flows.
During my internship at CEA Grenoble, Ivan Duchemin, Luigi Genovese, Éloïse Letournel, Antoine Levitt, and I developped a new method to compute the eigenvalues of a Schrödinger operator.

Teaching Activities

Activités d'enseignement

I am also a teaching assistant at École des Ponts ParisTech. I have given the following courses:

[-] Analysis of Partial Differential Equations , 34h, 24 students (first year ENPC), webpage. This course introduces several theoretical tools (such as Banach spaces, Hilbert spaces, Lebesgue spaces, Notion of Measure Theory, Fourier Analysis, Fourier Transform, Notion of Distribution Theory, ...) to handle PDE.
[-] Partial Differential Equations: Variational and Energetical Approaches, 15h, 25 students (first year ENPC), webpage. This course focuses on the mathematical analysis of various PDEs: from Poisson equation and variants, to non-symmetric equations (such as advection-diffusion problems) and vector-valued equations (such as linear elasticity).

Je suis également chargé de TD à l'École des Ponts ParisTech. J'ai donné les cours suivants :

[-] Analyse des Équations aux Dérivées Partielles, 34h, 24 étudiants (première année ENPC), page web. Ce cours introduit plusieurs outils théoriques (tels que les espaces de Banach, les espaces de Hilbert, les espaces de Lebesgue, les notions de théorie de la mesure, l'analyse de Fourier, la transformée de Fourier, les notions de théorie des distributions, ...) pour traiter les EDP.

Ce cours se concentre sur l'analyse mathématique de diverses EDP : de l'équation de Poisson et ses variantes, aux équations non symétriques (telles que les problèmes d'advection-diffusion) et aux équations vectorielles (telles que l'élasticité linéaire).
